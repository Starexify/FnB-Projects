Enchantments

Big Swords can be enhanced with a variety of enchantments to improve their combat performance. They are compatible with the following:

  • Sharpness - Increases overall damage dealt to all targets.
  • Smite - Increases damage dealt specifically to undead mobs.
  • Bane of Arthropods - Increases damage and applies Slowness to arthropod mobs.
  • Fire Aspect - Ignites the target upon impact, dealing fire damage over time.
  • Knockback - Increases the distance enemies are pushed back when struck.
  • Looting - Increases the quantity and quality of items dropped by mobs.
  • Sweeping Edge - Increases the damage of the sword's sweeping attack, improving crowd control.
  • Unbreaking - Provides a chance to avoid durability loss when the special attack is triggered.
  • Mending - Uses gathered Experience Orbs to restore the weapon's durability.

Variants

There are 11 unique types of shields available, each featuring two distinct tiers: Normal and Gilded. This creates a total of 22 shield variants for players to craft and master.

Every shield variant is constructed from specialized materials and possesses unique combat properties. While Normal shields provide foundational perks and specific weaknesses, the Gilded versions offer enhanced performance, such as higher proc chances for perks or reduced penalties for their inherent weaknesses.

The full registry of these 22 variants, including their icons and specific attributes, is detailed below:

Obtaining

Crafting Mechanics

Shields in Big Swords R are crafted through tier-based progression. Each material tier introduces stronger defensive properties, with recipes increasing in cost and effectiveness as players advance. All 11 base shields follow a standardized crafting layout, typically requiring the tier’s primary material arranged around a central core component.

Gilded Upgrading

Any standard shield can be upgraded into its Gilded variant. Gilded shields retain the original shield’s core behavior while significantly improving perk activation chances, nerf the weaknesses and buff the overall durability.

Gilded Recipe: Combine the Normal Shield variant with 6 Gold Ingots to obtain its Gilded counterpart. For some shields, the recipe requires another item instead of one of the gold ingots.

Usage

Blocking & Combat Perks

In Big Swords R, shields are not passive defense tools — blocking actively interacts with perks, weaknesses, and heavy-weapon combat. Proper timing and positioning are key. When blocking an attack:

  • Triggers shield-specific perks
  • May apply temporary weaknesses
  • Blocks the damage same as the normal minecraft vanilla shield
  • Heavy attacks from axes can set the shields on cooldown, similar to the vanilla one
  • Mistimed blocks may leave the player vulnerable during a short recovery window

Durability & Trade-offs

Shields in Big Swords R are designed with clear strengths and weaknesses. Higher-tier shields offer stronger perks and longer durability but may introduce mobility penalties or longer block cooldowns. Shields can be repaired using their primary material in an anvil or sustained with the Mending enchantment.

Enchantments

Shields can be enhanced with specific enchantments to increase their longevity in combat. They are compatible with the following:

  • Unbreaking - Reduces the chance of the shield taking durability damage when blocking attacks or projectiles.
  • Mending - Automatically restores the shield's durability by consuming gathered Experience Orbs.

Variants

There are 8 variants of glaives available, each with different materials and special attack ranges:

Obtaining

Glaives are crafted using their respective materials in a crafting table. They follow a similar pattern to other weapons but are designed for extended reach.

See the Individual Glaives section below for specific crafting recipes.

Usage

Attacking

Glaives can be used for standard melee attacks, dealing moderate damage with good reach. They are particularly effective against groups of enemies due to their sweeping attack pattern.

💡 Durability Tip: Glaives only lose durability when the swing attack successfully hits a target, making them more efficient than traditional weapons.

Special Attack

Glaives feature a unique charged attack mechanic. When you right-click and hold, the glaive charges up a special attack that can strike enemies from up to 5 blocks away. The damage dealt by this special attack varies by glaive tier and is shown in the weapon's tooltip.

Note: The glaive will not charge when looking at certain blocks (like creep blocks).

Biomass & Blood Collection

Glaives have special interactions with biomass-related mechanics:

🌱 Creep Block Interaction

Right-clicking on a Creep Block with a glaive allows you to "dig" the block, creating a suitable spot for planting biomass seeds.

🩸 Blood Collection

When holding a Vial in your off-hand, killing mobs with blood (excludes skeletons, iron golems, etc.) using the special attack will store their blood in the vial.

Obtaining

Scythes are crafted using their respective materials in a crafting table or smithing table. They feature a distinctive curved blade design optimized for sweeping attacks.

See the Individual Scythes section below for specific crafting recipes.

Usage

Attacking

Scythes are specialized weapons designed for area-of-effect combat. Their wide, sweeping attack pattern makes them particularly effective against groups of enemies. The curved blade allows scythes to hit multiple targets in a single swing, making them ideal for crowd control situations.

💫 Combat Tip: Scythes deal increased damage with their sweeping attacks compared to standard weapons, making them excellent for farming mobs or defending against hordes.

Special Attack

Scythes feature a unique charged attack mechanic similar to glaives. When you right-click and hold, the scythe charges up a special reaping attack. This powerful strike can hit enemies from a distance and deals devastating damage. The special attack damage varies by scythe tier and is displayed in the weapon's tooltip.

⚔️ Note: The charged reaping attack has a unique animation and can cleave through multiple enemies in its path, making it extremely effective for crowd control.

Enchantments

Scythes are unique hybrid tools that utilize both utility and combat-focused enchantments. They are compatible with the following:

  • Soul Stealer - A specialized enchantment that grants a chance for enemies to drop Souls upon death.

Blood Vial

The Blood Vial is an essential tool for the biomass farming system. It allows you to collect blood from mobs and use it to create biomass-related items.

Crafting

Blood Collection

To collect blood, you must hold a Blood Vial in your off-hand and kill mobs that have blood (excludes skeletons, iron golems, and other bloodless entities) using a Glaive's special attack. When you successfully kill a mob with blood, the vial will fill with their blood.

Blood Collection Process

⚔️ Important: You must use a glaive's charged special attack to collect blood. Regular attacks will not fill the vial. See the Glaives page for more information.

Biomass Farming

Biomass farming is a multi-step process that involves creating specialized blocks and seeds to grow biomass. This material can then be used to craft powerful equipment.

Creating Biomass Seeds

To create Biomass Seeds, hold a Blood Vial in your off-hand and Torchflower Seeds in your main hand, then right-click. This will consume the blood vial and convert the seeds into Biomass Seeds.

Creating Creep Ball

To create a Creep Ball, hold a Blood Vial in your off-hand and a Slimeball in your main hand, then right-click. This will consume the blood vial and convert the slimeball into a Creep Ball.

Creating Creep Block

Creep Blocks are created by righ-clicking with a Creep Ball on a Soul Sand Block.

Biomass Block

Once you've harvested biomass, you can create Biomass Blocks for compact storage. These are crafted using 9 Biomass in a crafting table, and can be broken back down into 9 Biomass when needed.

Planting Biomass

To plant Biomass Seeds, you must first prepare a Creep Block by right-clicking on it with a Glaive. This will "dig" the block, creating a suitable planting spot. You can then plant your Biomass Seeds in the prepared block.

Souls

Obtaining Souls

Souls can be obtained by killing mobs with souls while using a weapon enchanted with Soul Stealer. When a mob with a soul is killed with this enchantment, there's a chance it will drop its soul.

📖 Soul Stealer Enchantment: See the Enchantments page for detailed information about Soul Stealer drop chances per level.

Soul Sand Transformation

When a Soul is dropped onto a Sand Block, it will transform the sand into Soul Sand. This provides an alternative way to obtain soul sand without needing to venture into the Nether.

💡 Tip: This interaction makes soul sand renewable and easily farmable in the Overworld.

Livingmetal Ingot

Livingmetal Ingots are crafted by surrounding an Iron Ingot with 4 Souls in a crafting table.

Soul Stealer

Scythe-exclusive enchantment

Usage

Soul Stealer is an exclusive enchantment that can only be applied to Scythes. When you kill an entity using a Scythe's special attack with this enchantment, you have a chance to receive the entity's soul as a drop.

⚠️ Important: Soul Stealer only works when killing entities with the Scythe's special attack (charged right-click attack). Regular attacks will not drop souls.

🚫 Undead Exclusion: Soul Stealer does not work on undead mobs such as Zombies, Skeletons, Phantoms, Drowned, Husks, Strays, Wither Skeletons, Zombie Villagers, Zombified Piglins, and other undead entities.

Enchantment Levels

Soul Stealer has three levels, with each level increasing the chance of obtaining a soul by 30%:

Level Enchantment Name Soul Drop Chance
I Soul Stealer I 30%
II Soul Stealer II 60%
III Soul Stealer III 90%

💡 Tip: At Soul Stealer III, you have a 90% chance to obtain a soul from each valid kill, making soul farming highly efficient for crafting Livingmetal equipment.
